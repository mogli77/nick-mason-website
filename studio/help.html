How to use Studio

The portfolio page, freely editable. Five minutes to learn everything.

Studio shows the real portfolio page. Everything on it — every photo, every video — can be picked up and moved, resized, cropped, layered, or deleted. When it looks right, you publish, and the live site updates about a minute later. Nothing is permanent: every change can be undone, and every publish can be rolled back.

Getting in

  • Open /studio/ on the preview site, enter the passphrase, and pick your name (that's how changes get labeled in the history).
  • The first time it opens it may say it's converting the layout — that just makes everything movable. The page looks exactly the same.

Moving things

  • Click a photo to select it. Drag it anywhere.
  • Pink lines appear when it lines up with other photos — it gently snaps to them.
  • Drop a photo on top of another to overlap them. The little toolbar's ↥ / ↧ buttons control which one is in front.
  • Arrow keys nudge the selection a little; hold Shift for bigger steps.

Resizing

  • Corner handles make a photo bigger or smaller (its shape stays the same).
  • Side handles stretch it wider or taller — the photo fills the new shape, and you choose what shows with Crop.

Cropping

  • Double-click a photo (or hit Crop on its toolbar).
  • The full photo appears faded around the frame — drag it to choose what stays visible. The faded part is what gets hidden. Click anywhere else when done.
  • If nothing happens, the frame already shows the whole photo — stretch it with a side handle first, then crop.
  • Made a mess? Select the photo and hit Reset crop in the Selected panel.

Groups: select several at once

  • Drag on empty background to draw a box — everything it touches gets selected. (Shift-click adds or removes one at a time.)
  • Drag any selected photo and the whole group moves together.
  • ⌫ Delete removes the selection (or use the Delete button in the panel).
The magic move — slide & push: hold ⌥ Option while dragging a selection up or down. It moves as one block, a blue line shows where it will land, and when you let go the rest of the page politely slides out of the way and re-stacks. This is how you reorder whole sections of the page.

Zooming out (seeing the whole page)

  • Pinch on the trackpad (or hold ⌘ and scroll) anywhere on the page — zoom out as far as you like, Miro-style.
  • Or use the 10% / 25% / 50% / 100% buttons in the top bar.
  • Everything still works zoomed out: select a whole region, ⌥-drag it somewhere else, then zoom back in to fine-tune.
  • The strip on the left is a mini-map of the entire page — click anywhere on it to jump there.

Adding photos and videos

  • The right panel holds every photo in the library, sorted by project, with search.
  • Drag one onto the page — it lands where you drop it, at its natural shape.
  • Videos live at the top of the panel and work exactly the same way.

Checking your work

  • Preview (top right) opens your current arrangement as the real page in a new tab — animations and all — without publishing anything.
  • Mobile view shows how phones will see it: photos stack top-to-bottom in reading order automatically. You never have to design the mobile version.

Undo, drafts, and safety

  • ⌘Z undoes, ⇧⌘Z redoes — every move, resize, crop, or delete.
  • Your work saves as a draft in your browser automatically. Close the tab, come back tomorrow, it offers to resume.
  • If two people edit at once, Studio notices at publish time and asks who wins — nothing gets silently overwritten.

Publishing

  • Hit Publish, optionally add a note (these notes reach Claude — write things like "rearranged the middle, please polish the spacing"), and confirm.
  • The live page updates in about a minute.
  • Every publish is saved in the site's history forever — any version can be brought back.

Pro mode (Nick's local version)

Run netlify dev --port 8890 in the site folder and open localhost:8890/studio/. Studio detects it's local and unlocks extra power:

  • Save writes straight to portfolio.html on disk — instant, nothing deployed. Iterate freely, check the page on the dev server.
  • Commit & push when ready — that's what actually deploys.
  • Drop image files from Finder onto the photo panel to add brand-new photos to the library (they're included in the next commit).
  • + Add video by URL for new Cloudinary videos.
  • Numeric position/size fields, ⌘D duplicate, and align/distribute buttons for multi-selections.

If something looks wrong

  • Weird state? Reload the page — you'll be offered your draft back.
  • Draft itself looks wrong? Decline it and Studio reloads the live version fresh.
  • Published something bad? Tell Claude "revert the last Studio publish" — one command.
